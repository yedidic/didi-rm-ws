About Didi

Presence, curiosity and a human meeting

I’m Didi Cohen, a Rosen Method Bodywork intern based in Rehavia, Jerusalem.

I began studying Rosen Method in 2019, completed the training program and began my internship in 2023. I hold a BSW in Social Work from Bar-Ilan University and also work as a software developer—different worlds that have each taught me something about listening, complexity and change.

I’m married to Maayan and father to Pelly Fani. Alongside individual Rosen Method sessions, I am a Kundalini Yoga teacher and teacher trainer. Together with Maayan, I care for Beit Shel Yoga, a Jerusalem studio and community where I teach a weekly group and offer private classes.

Rosen Method Bodywork

A gentle practice that combines attentive touch and conversation to invite awareness of chronic muscular tension and patterns in our lives.

A session takes place on a massage table. Touch responds, listens and makes space. There is no goal you have to achieve; sensations, breath, words or quiet can each be part of the meeting. Rosen Method is a complementary wellbeing practice and is not a replacement for medical or mental-health care.
